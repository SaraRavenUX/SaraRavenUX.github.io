Case Study 1 — The No-Go Decision

The Situation

J.P. Morgan Chase planned to launch a complex tiered interest rate CD product for affluent customers — without a single conversation with those customers. My skip-level manager secured 4 weeks to test the premise. I led the research.

My Role

I architected the research strategy and led a cross-functional team of one researcher, two designers, a content designer, and product partners. I also coached my manager and skip-level on how to frame findings for senior leadership — translating research into executive narrative.

Team & Timeline

1 researcher 2 designers 1 content designer Multiple product partners 4 weeks end-to-end

The Approach

WK 1

Competitive Analysis

No other bank offered a tiered interest rate CD. The absence was itself a signal.

WK 2

Branch Interviews

25–30 affluent customers across two segments: rate chasers and brand loyals.

WK 3

Prototype Testing

Content, visuals & a sliding calculator tested with employees, then customers.

WK 4

Synthesis & Delivery

Findings synthesized into a leadership-ready No Go recommendation.

Case Study 1 — Key Findings

What Thirty Conversations Confirmed

No other competitors
offered a comparable tiered-rate CD product

Competitive analysis found no precedent across 6 major national banks. Branch interviews with 25–30 affluent customers — split between rate chasers and brand-loyal account holders — revealed a market that wasn't asking for this product and wasn't equipped to evaluate it.

"I don't think about whether my rate is competitive. I trust this bank and I've never had a reason to leave."
— Brand-loyal participant
1

Two segments. One flawed premise.

Rate chasers managed savings as financial instruments and would move $150K+ for basis points. Brand loyals had never comparison-shopped their CD rate. Neither segment was seeking a complex tiered structure.

2

Complexity eroded confidence.

27 of 30 participants required researcher guidance to navigate to their correct tier. Regulatory disclosure language actively undermined trust — participants stopped reading before reaching the rate table.

3

The signal was an intersection of findings.

The combination of an account that was not comparable to the best rates in the market, an incredibly confusing product experience, a segment of customers that would leave regardless, and another segment that would stay loyal regardless — together, these were the strongest evidence of a no-go decision.

Case Study 2 — The Notification Cascade

The Situation

At J.P. Morgan Chase, fees were the #1 reason customers called after receiving an overdraft alert — 22% of all complaints. The overdraft journey triggered 3 of 6 highest-call-volume communications and 50%+ of consumer alert servicing costs. I led a 4-week, three-workstream research program to understand why.

01   Customer Journey Mapping
End-to-end journey maps for Account Overdrawn (AO), Balance Below Threshold (BBT) & Notice of Insufficient Funds (NIF) alerts across 4 overdraft scenarios — revealing a cascade of inconsistent, actionless messages across push, SMS, email, and in-app channels.
02   Voice of Customer Analysis
Thematic analysis and text mining across ~13K verbatims. 375 statistically representative complaints coded across 7 categories and 10 products.
03   Customer Interviews
11 participants via UserTesting (8 moderated, 3 unmoderated). UMUX-lite usability scores plus structured content assessment of all alert types and the NIF letter.
My role: Led the full program; managed and coached a junior researcher on my team through her first large-scale multi-method study.

Case Study 2 — Outcome

The Finding

Customers weren't failing to read their notifications — they were receiving the wrong content, in the wrong order, with no actionable guidance, often too late to act. The cascade was a product of fragmented communication design across channels.

"It says 'we paid or returned.' Those are two very different things — they aren't being specific to my situation."
— Interview participant
"I'm an attorney. I'm not afraid of regulatory language... but the letter tells me more than I care to know."
— Interview participant
What This Demonstrates
  • Systems-level research thinking
  • Multi-method synthesis
  • Mentoring junior researchers
  • Translating complexity into a prioritized roadmap

The Impact

✓

Research elevated to inform cross-product notification standards across J.P. Morgan Chase's consumer banking portfolio

✓

✓

Findings used to secure resourcing for a broader omnichannel alert strategy, extending the program's scope beyond overdraft

Case Study 3 — Amplitude Intercept Program

The Capability

Amplitude intercept surveys surface in-app immediately after a user completes a journey, capturing real, recent experience at scale. I built cohort-targeting workflows with FanDuel's analytics team to reach precise user segments — producing statistically reliable insights in days rather than weeks.

01   Competitive Benchmarking
Quarterly  ·  2,390+ responses  ·  3 competitors
"How does FanDuel's journey experience compare — and how is it trending?"
Approach:
Amplitude intercept (ease + CSAT) paired with dscout head-to-head usability panels across Deposit, Withdrawal, Login & Account Management.
Outcome:
Findings shared with senior leadership quarterly. Wave 3 complete; Wave 4 targets Login friction & onboarding abandonment.
02   KY Derby FTD Dropoff
Event-triggered  ·  36K+ sign-ups tracked
"Why aren't new sign-ups converting to first deposits after a tentpole event?"
Approach:
Amplitude cohort of non-depositing sign-ups within 14 days of the event. Intercept survey triangulated with funnel data and CS chat analysis.
Outcome:
Product deprioritized FTD prompt in onboarding; replaced with educational content to build readiness before asking for commitment.
03   MySpend Email Summaries
Two cohorts  ·  1,196 responses
"Would a monthly MySpend email drive engagement — including for lapsed users?"
Approach:
Two Amplitude cohorts (recent + lapsed, 30–90 days). Correlation analysis tested whether interest predicted behavioral intent beyond stated preference.
Outcome:
No statistically significant difference between cohorts. Email program greenlit for both simultaneously. Prototyping sprint approved.

Research Leadership

How I Build Research Practice

1

Cultures of rigor on UX teams

I instill high standards from day one — coaching researchers on synthesis, reviewing for methodological quality, and preparing them to present with confidence to senior stakeholders.

2

Closing the gap between research and data

I build Amplitude cohorts, test tagged events, and ground research in behavioral reality alongside analytics partners. This fluency makes output more credible and creates cross-disciplinary mentoring opportunities.

3

Stakeholder relationships that make research matter

Research only changes decisions if the right people trust it. I align early with product and design partners, communicate in the language of business risk, and earn a seat at the table before priorities are set.

4

Operating across the full research ecosystem

Beyond core product work: I build compliance SOPs, legal review processes, and advocate for accessible design. I view research leadership as responsibility for the health of the practice itself — not just individual study quality.

5

AI-augmented research: more rigor, not less

I integrate AI tools across the full research lifecycle — not to replace judgment, but to multiply capacity. This means more studies, tighter timelines, and outputs that hold up to scrutiny. My 15+ years of research experience is what makes AI integration safe: I can rapidly member-check, validate, and course-correct AI outputs before they reach stakeholders or shape decisions.
